    Recognize Fake News & Misinformation

    At NetAware, we educate you on the dangers of fake news and misinformation in the digital age. According to UNICEF (2023), misinformation is false or inaccurate information that is spread, regardless of intent to deceive. Learn to identify these threats and implement best practices to combat misinformation that can mislead, polarize, and undermine trust in reliable sources (University of Victoria Libraries, 2023).

    Types of Misinformation

    1. Fake News

    Completely fabricated stories designed to deceive readers, often for political or financial gain.

    Learn More

    2. Satire

    Humorous or exaggerated content that may be mistaken for real news due to its style.

    Learn More

    3. Propaganda

    Biased information spread to promote a political cause or ideology.

    Learn More

    4. Clickbait

    Sensational headlines designed to attract clicks, often leading to misleading content.

    Learn More

    5. Deepfakes

    AI-generated fake videos or audio that appear authentic, spreading false narratives.

    Learn More

    Best Practices for Avoiding Fake News

    1. Read from a Variety of Sources

    Consult multiple reliable news outlets to get a balanced view and avoid echo chambers.

    Learn More

    2. Be Aware of Your Own Biases

    Recognize personal biases that may influence how you interpret information.

    Learn More

    3. Take Time to Think Before Sharing

    Pause and evaluate the information critically before deciding to share it.

    Learn More

    4. Don't Just Share Online

    Verify information through multiple channels before spreading it digitally.
