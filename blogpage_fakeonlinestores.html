    Spot Fake Online Stores

    At NetAware, we help you identify and avoid fake online stores to protect your finances and personal information. Fake websites can look legitimate but are designed to steal your data or money. By learning the red flags and best practices from Republic Bank (n.d.), you can shop safely online.

    Signs of Fake Online Stores

    1. Poor Website Design

    Legitimate stores have professional, polished websites. Fake sites often have low-quality images, typos, or inconsistent branding.

    Learn More

    2. Suspicious URLs

    Check for HTTPS and avoid sites with misspellings or unusual domain extensions that don't match the brand.

    Learn More

    3. No Contact Information

    Real stores provide clear contact details like phone numbers, addresses, or email. Fake sites often lack this or use generic info.

    Learn More

    4. Too-Good-to-Be-True Deals

    Unrealistic discounts or limited-time offers are common tactics to lure victims into fake sites.

    Learn More

    Tips to Spot and Avoid Fake Online Stores

    1. Research the Seller

    Check reviews on independent sites like Trustpilot or BBB. Search for the store's name plus "scam" to see if there are complaints.

    Learn More

    2. Use Secure Payment Methods

    Pay with credit cards for buyer protection or use services like PayPal. Avoid wire transfers or gift cards.

    Learn More

    3. Verify SSL Certificates

    Ensure the site has a padlock icon and HTTPS in the URL to confirm encrypted connections.

    Learn More

    4. Read Return Policies

    Legitimate stores have clear return and refund policies. Fake sites may have vague or non-existent policies.

    Learn More

    5. Trust Your Instincts

    If something feels off, like pressure to buy quickly or requests for unnecessary personal info, walk away.
