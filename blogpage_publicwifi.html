    Safe Use of Public Wifi

    At NetAware, we guide you on safely navigating public Wi-Fi networks to protect your personal data and privacy. Public Wi-Fi can be convenient, but it poses significant risks such as man-in-the-middle attacks and eavesdropping (San Diego State University, n.d.; Washington Technology Solutions, n.d.). By following best practices from these sources, you can minimize these threats and stay secure online.

    Risks of Public Wifi

    1. Man-in-the-Middle Attacks

    Attackers can intercept data between your device and the network, stealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

    Learn More

    2. Eavesdropping

    Unsecured networks allow others to monitor your online activity, including websites visited and data transmitted.

    Learn More

    3. Malware Distribution

    Public hotspots can be hotspots for malware, where infected devices spread viruses or ransomware to connected users.

    Learn More

    4. Fake Hotspots

    Rogue access points mimic legitimate networks to trick users into connecting, leading to data theft.

    Learn More

    Tips for Safe Use of Public Wifi

    1. Use a VPN

    Encrypt your internet traffic with a reputable VPN to protect data from interception on public networks.

    Learn More

    2. Avoid Sensitive Activities

    Refrain from online banking, shopping, or accessing personal accounts on public Wi-Fi; save these for secure networks.

    Learn More

    3. Enable Firewall and Keep Software Updated

    Turn on your device's firewall and ensure all software is up-to-date to block vulnerabilities exploited on public networks.

    Learn More

    4. Use HTTPS and Turn Off Sharing

    Stick to websites with HTTPS, and disable file sharing and Bluetooth to prevent unauthorized access to your device.

    Learn More

    5. Log Out and Forget Networks

    Always log out of accounts after use and forget the network to avoid automatic reconnections in the future.

    Learn More
