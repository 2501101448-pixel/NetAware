Recognize Online Scams

Stay alert, stay smart — don’t fall victim to online tricks.

Common Types of Online Scams

1. Phishing Emails

Scammers send fake emails that look real to steal your login credentials or bank details. Always double-check the sender’s address.

2. Fake Online Stores

Beware of online shops with too-good-to-be-true offers. Check reviews and secure payment methods before buying.

3. Investment Scams

Fraudsters promise big returns with little risk. Avoid sending money to unknown companies or individuals.

4. Romance Scams

Scammers pretend to build relationships online and then ask for money. Never send money to someone you haven’t met.

5. Lottery or Prize Scams

You receive a message claiming you won a prize — but need to pay a “fee” first. Real prizes never require payment to claim.

Examples of Scam Messages

Example 1: Fake Bank Email

An email says “Your account is locked. Click here to verify.” Real banks never ask for personal info through email links.

Example 2: Fake Login Page

Scammers make websites that look like Facebook or Gmail to steal your password. Always check the web address before logging in.

How to Avoid Online Scams

  • ✅ Check the sender’s email or website URL carefully.
  • ✅ Never share passwords or OTPs with anyone.
  • ✅ Use official apps and websites only.
  • ✅ Report suspicious messages immediately.
